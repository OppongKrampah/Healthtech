About My Topic:

The healthylife style of individuals has been a problem for many communities in recent years, whether being low income communities or area with high standards of living. Ensuring the health of all individuals is a crucial aspect of the human life, and has been one of the United Nations goals to reach by 2030. In places like in Africa, where the lack of healthcare is a major problem, there are diseases like malaria, Yellow Fever, cholera and many more and it's partly beacuse of the lack of healthcare, less motivation my parents to get their wards vaccinated. Also, there is ignorance about health issues, like good nutrition and sex education, which ultimately lead to adverse outcomes with deterimental effects on individuals well-being and increased teenage pregnancies.

According to the National Institute of Health, findings show that no West African beat the minimum health priority of 15%. Ghana (8.43%), Carpe Verde (8.29%), and Burkina Faso (7.60%) were the top three countries with the highest average health priority index, while Guinea (3.05%), and others had the least health priority in the West Africa within the period of our analysis (2010 to 2018). According to UNICEF, Vaccinations from 2019-2021 reveals that 12.7 million children in Africa missed out on one or more vaccinations over three years. Nigeria and Ethiopia are the two countries with the largest number of zero-dose children on the continent with more than 2.2 million and 1.1 million respectively. In 2022, 34 of the 54 countries in Africa experienced disease outbreaks such as measles, cholera and poliovirus.
